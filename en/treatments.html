Treatments

Quality and personal attention are our focus. We therefore set clear boundaries between the treatments that may be performed by dentists, by specialists and by the dental hygienist or the prevention assistant. As a patient, you know what you can expect from us. Both dentists are general practitioners with a broad orientation. This means that you can come to us for all your usual oral care, such as oral hygiene, conservative dentistry (fillings, crowns, bridges), prosthetic dentistry (including dentures and frame prostheses), endodontics (root canal treatments), periodontics (treatment of gum disease).

You can also turn to us for affordable cosmetic and aesthetic dentistry.

We pay special attention to anxious patients and have a long experience with the treatment of this group.

Regular periodic dental check-ups are important for the health of your mouth. We will then discuss daily oral care with you so that your teeth and soft tissue of the mouth remain healthy. If necessary, we take action to improve oral hygiene.

If the dentist detects problems in your teeth, he or she will discuss with you whether additional treatment is necessary. The dentist also informs you about:

  • the number and duration of treatments
  • the expected results
  • the prognosis and the possible risks of the treatments

Information about dental treatments can be found in the information about oral health on www.allesoverhetgebit.nl (in Dutch only)

The dentist will immediately discuss minor procedures with you and the treatment will follow shortly if necessary. This is, for example, the case with simple dental cleaning or making a small filling. With more extensive treatments we inform you about the costs. If you wish, the treatment plan with the associated costs can be recorded in writing. Partly because of the legal obligation, we always do this for treatments that cost more than € 150. The relevant treatments are only carried out after your approval. This is generally possible within 15 working days.

If you have to undergo special treatment, you do not have to look for the specialist in question yourself. We can refer you to our colleagues for dental surgery, orthodontics, implantology, periodontology (gum disease), gnathology (specialists for the jaw joint), endodontology (specialists for root canal treatments) and maxillofacial prosthetics (special prostheses). We also work together with a well-equipped dental technician within walking distance of the practice so that repairs or extensions to prostheses can take place within one day.

We store the information about your teeth and the history of treatments in our computer. This system is, of course, well protected and backed up professionally every day. We keep the data for at least 15 years. At your (written) request, we can also send your data (electronically) to another practitioner.

Regular checks

In our dental practice, the prevention of dental problems has the highest priority. Prevention is simply better, easier and cheaper than cure. We are happy to ensure that your teeth are clean and your gums remain healthy.

Prevention for children

We strive for healthy teeth for all children! We therefore advise you to take your child to the dentist from the age of two when you go for a check-up yourself. Treatment is usually not necessary at this age. You mainly receive information and advice from the dentist or the prevention assistant. If you then have your child's teeth checked every six months, any problems can be identified and corrected in time.

We use sealing and fluoride to prevent caries and dental erosion. However, the most important thing is our cleaning instructions and nutritional advice. We give this not only to the little ones, but also to children who are undergoing the “orthodontic treatment”, because it is always difficult to clean sufficiently between the braces.

Oral hygiene

We are happy to help you with the extra care of your teeth and gums. To this end, the dentist also examines the gums on the basis of the so-called DPSI score. For example, if you suffer from bleeding, inflamed and / or withdrawn gums, loose teeth and the DPSI score is 3 or higher, then you are probably suffering from periodontitis.

You can also come to us for the treatment of bad breath (halitosis).

Periodontitis

Periodontitis can have several causes. A common cause is the presence of bacteria. If necessary, we can use a simple test to determine whether and, if so, which bacteria are present in the mouth. The treatment plan is drawn up and discussed with you on the basis of the test results. If necessary, we will send you to a specialist for gum disease (periodontist).

Fillings

In our practice we use high-quality composite filling materials in the color of the teeth. Advantages of the tooth-colored fillings are:

  1. they are adapted to the color of the tooth and are therefore less noticeable than the gray amalgam fillings;
  2. the tooth-colored filling material is stuck to the tooth or molar, so that not healthy parts of the tooth or molar need to be ground away unnecessarily (which is necessary for an amalgam filling);
  3. the adhesive technique also ensures good adhesion of the filling to the tooth or molar, making it stronger.

Tooth-colored fillings also have disadvantages. Not all tooth-colored fillings are as strong as amalgam and break down sooner. That is why they are sometimes not used for large fillings in the molars. Applying a tooth-colored filling is also more laborious than an amalgam filling. More actions are also needed for repairing teeth or molars with tooth-colored filling material. This takes more time, which entails higher costs. After applying a tooth-colored filling, the teeth may be more sensitive for some time, especially at the transition from warm to cold and vice versa or when the relevant teeth are loaded. That has to do with a tension between the filling material and the natural tooth or molar. The larger the filling, the more sensitive it can be.

Crowns & bridges and implants

In some cases it may be necessary for a tooth or molar to need a crown, for example if a filling becomes too large and no longer has a hold, or if a tooth or molar has previously had a root canal treatment and has therefore become too vulnerable, or sometimes for aesthetic reasons.

A crown is a kind of shell made from porcelain, zirconium or metal, or a combination of these materials. A crown protects a tooth or molar and gives it support and stability.

If one or more teeth are missing, a bridge can be a good choice to replace the missing elements. A bridge consists of at least two crowns (pillar) and one or more bridge intermediate parts (dummies) that are connected to each other. The dentist can use an x-ray to assess whether the pillar teeth are suitable for a bridge.

Another way to replace missing elements in your teeth is through an implant. The choice of a bridge or implant depends on a number of factors such as your general health condition, dental situation, bone level and personal preference. The available budget also plays a role.

If you need a cast restoration, make an appointment for a consultation. After anamnesis, X-ray, extensive examination and a personal interview, your wishes and possibilities are discussed by the dentist.

Dentures

You can contact us for both full and partial dental prostheses, including prostheses on implants and frame prostheses. For repairs and filling of prostheses, you can bring your teeth before 10:00 in the morning and it will be ready the same day later in the afternoon. For filling and for expansion and some repairs an impression must be taken.

Extractions

The pulling of teeth / molars and minor surgery can be done in our own practice. For more complex treatments you are referred to the dental surgeon or another specialist.

Endodontics

In our practice we regularly perform root canal treatments. These are also called channel or nerve treatment. During a root canal treatment, the dentist removes the inflamed tissue from the dental pulp.

The cause of the inflammation is caused by bacteria that have penetrated into your tooth and into the dental nerve (pulp) due to tooth decay, a leaking filling or a severe blow.

Inflamed tooth tissue can no longer heal. It is an irreversible process. The inflamed tissue must be removed. To do this, your dentist first opens your tooth or molar and removes the inflamed tissue. He / she then cleans the canal with small files and rinses it with a disinfecting rinsing fluid. Finally, the canals are filled. Your dentist often takes one or more X-rays during treatment. This way he / she has good control over the course of the treatment.

After the root canal treatment, your dentist will build your tooth or close again with a filling. If the tooth is weakened, a crown may be needed. A tooth or molar treated in this way can last for a long time.

Night guard / anti-grinding protection

If you wake up in the morning with muscle pain in your jaws or if you have symptoms such as pain in the jaw joints and / or headache, it is quite possible that you will grind your teeth at night. Moreover, excessive movement of the teeth and molars at night results in wear of the teeth and toothache. This can be prevented by a night guard that is custom made for you.

Sportguard

We manufacture mouth guards for all kinds of sports, from kickboxing to hockey. In addition to the best protection, a custom-made protector also offers the best wearing comfort.

Teeth whitening

The huge range of bleaches and treatments is confusing. Many products promise beautiful white teeth, but unfortunately that is different in practice. An unsafe bleaching process can even damage your teeth. If you are considering a bleaching treatment, choose a safe and professional method, under the supervision of your dentist.

The legislation has changed since October 2012. As a result, effective bleaching treatment can only be prescribed by a dentist. Feel free to ask for advice on your next visit to the practice.
